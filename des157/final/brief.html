Project Brief

The project topic will be improving the "Every Picture"
studio project.

NIGHTRUNNER INTERNATIONAL

Seeing the images at the beginning in a slideshow
in small intervals is a nice feature to capture
a user's attention. I can see that this website's
target audience are car fanatics and how it advertises
their products and have a "Featured Items" section.
Some of these features nice and I really like the
images used to help describe their website. I would
improve more on the images to be more
interactive and eye catching than just in a small
contained area. Overall, I would like to redesign
my project to be simplistic as this, using
my own images and without the use of an
external engine and that I can use more of
my own images to speak about who I am.
I plan on keeping the old images, but reworking
a newer design and add more images to it.

STORYBIRD

This website, similar to the one above, also
has a main component to capture the attention
of users that come to the site. I really
get capture easily by big images because
I am curious to see what it does next
or how it plays a part of the site.
This website looks to be targetted towards writers.
Knowing the target audience can really help with
finding out what to put on a page and
how to place components on a page to get
users to interact with them and make it
somewhat fun rather than it being bland.
The different panels and the different books
and their description is something I really
like, an idea I would want to implement
to my project and update accordingly.

Project Goals

My goals for the project is to simulate some
usage of a slide show component on the landing page
and capture the user's attention right off the bat.
Alongside that, I do want to have my images
in a zig-zag pattern and having a brief explanation
to what it is, and have a modal appear
with the image and full description displayed
after the user clicks on the box. It will
be similar in terms of the slide and zig-zag
idea, but it will be different with my images
and including a modal for a temporary screen
to display the image and full description.

Design Strategies

The overall look I want to aim for is
minimalistic with some classy dark colors.
I want to keep the theme in black and white
with gradient effects on the image and
have transitional animations on some of the
interactive components. Hovering a mouse
over the images can also fade it into the
next image in that category too. It can
be multiple of the same subject and have
a dedicated slideshow to it. This can be
repeated for different images tiles as well.

Interaction Strategies

Interacting with the images, some I wish to
behave similarly to the sunset feature I did
previously on my "Every Picture" Project. I
thought it was really cool to have a mouse
drag across a screen be the controller to
show a change on the screen updated live.
The modals would show as a feedback to the
user to know that clicking an image opens
another screen, and I might implement a
like button for the user to let me know
if they like the image or not. This will
also provide feedback for me as well,
letting me know if I can make it better
or if this user is interested in it.

Experience Goals

The experience for the audience is that
I want them to know who I am and
what my life is like and how my views
are. I want them to know my thoughts are
based off what they can see in an image.
I want the audience to have a good time
browsing around and understanding more
about me and my lifestyle or my hobbies
or the important aspects of my life.
I'm not entirely sure on how I will
achieve this, but I will work hard on
it and develop more on top of it
as I continue to work on it.
